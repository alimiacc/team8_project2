Impact of COVID-19 on US Oil Production, Consumption, and Price.

Using the interactive charts below to explore the dataset

By: Andrew Bartczak, Derek Teed, Nisha Sapkota, and Alimi Mukaila

After the outbreak of COVID-19 in the US, a huge downfall in demand for petroleum products has been witnessed which results in a downfall in oil prices. Due to lockdown and travel restrictions for the aviation industry to other transportation services such as buses, cars, people are staying at home and the demand for oil has also been decreased. Furthermore, various onshore oil companies storage are full, reducing workers for COVID safety, most of them are closing their companies and are bankrupt because of the low demand for oil.
